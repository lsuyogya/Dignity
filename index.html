Who We Are

About Dignity
Initiative

Dignity Initiative is a Kathmandu-based not-for-profit, not-government organization, whichworks on knowledge production on the issues of social exclusion and human rights with a special focus one Dalit. The Dalit community shares 13.2 percent of the total national population of Nepal. They are politically excluded, economically deprived, and socio-culturally discriminated. They have been struggling for more than seventy years to claim Dalit as equal as other human beings.

Leading With Purpose

Dalits in Data

This is a data-driven age and the importance of data has increased significantly. However, marginalized communities like Dalit continue to suffer from data discrimination and censorship. It was found that the data especially of marginalized communities has always been manipulated, distorted and hidden by government to limit the redistribution of resources and opportunities. The new constitution of Nepal has pledged many rights to Dalit. These commitments should be translated into policies, programs and budgetary allocations. In addition, Nepal is a party to numbers of international Human Rights instruments and mechanism, but it lacks appropriate data. This is often resulted into twisting of achievement and progress made by the government. Therefore, Dignity Initiative conducts lobby and advocacy with the government to generate disaggregated data of Dalit. Dignity Initiative also presents Dalit related data using info-graphics to various audiences in order to maximize the potential for data-driven insights.

Capacity Building

Continuous learning is the prerequisite for social change and transformation. Dignity Initiative conducts trainings and capacity building activities to empower upcoming youths with research knowledge and writing skills on a host of issues that matters to Dalit. This further intends toprovide platforms to produce experts from Dalit community in the specific field.
